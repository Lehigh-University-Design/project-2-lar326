Fold an Origami Flower

Origami flowers symbolize everlasting friendship and relationship. Flowers wilt over time, but an origami flower does not. A fun way to show someone that your love for them is everlasting is gifting them an origami flower!

The Lily Flower

In Chinese culture, lilies are known to symbolize good luck and long lasting love. In addition to their usage at religious ceremonies, lilies have also been used for medicinal purposes.

Time: 5 minutes

Supplies:

  • Any size paper

  • A flat surface to work on

Difficulty Level: Beginner

1 Folding the Base Shape

Step One

** Note: Hover over the image to play video!**

Fold the bottom point up to the top point. Crease the paper, then unfold.

Step Two

Fold the left point across to the right point. Crease the paper, then unfold.

Step Three

Fold the bottom edge to the top. Crease well, then unfold.

Step Four

Fold the left edge to the right. Crease well, then unfold.

Step Five

Fold the center side points in to the center bottom point, then flatten the paper.

** Note: This is called a waterbomb base. **

Step Six

Take the front right flap, open it and squash it aligned with the middle line.

Step Seven

Repeat on all four flaps.

2 Folding the Petals

Step One

On the top face, fold the side corners to the middle line, crease, then unfold.

Step Two

Fold the top point down to the bottom point. Crease well, then unfold.

Step Three

Using the creases made in the last two steps, fold the bottom point of only the top layer to the top point. At the same time, reverse the side folds in, and flatten.

Step Four

Repeat section 3 steps one, two, and three, for all four sides.

Step Five

On each of the four faces, fold the top triangle down to create a diamond shape. Then rotate so the open part is at the top.

Step Six

On the top face, fold the side corners to the middle line and flatten.

Step Seven

Repeat on all four faces.

3 Shaping the Petals

Step One

On each face, fold the top point out to form the petals.

Step Two

To create a curling effect in the petal, roll each petal over a pencil and hold for a few seconds.

Step Three

Your origami lily is complete!
